Consumer Rebate Program

The CRP offers rebates to residential customers to promote the efficient use of energy and water. The program helps customers reduce their bills, save resources, protect the environment, and improve the comfort of their home.

Apply Today

Great news! Heat Pump rebates are increasing for equipment purchased and installed on or after November 1, 2025. Heat Pump Water Heaters will be eligible for up to $2,500* per unit and Heat Pump HVAC systems will be eligible for up to $2,500* per ton.

*Rebate amount is based on gallon capacity/UEF, or SEER2/HSPF2 rating.

About the Program

The Consumer Rebate Program (CRP) is designed to both educate and encourage the use of the most energy-efficient products by providing rebates for products that exceed state/federal efficiency standards, ranging from variable speed pool pump and motors to home heating ventilation and air conditioning (HVAC) systems, such as a central air conditioner

Installing energy-efficient equipment can help you reduce your bills, save resources, protect the environment, and improve the comfort of your home. Apply for the following rebates today!

Eligibility Requirements

  • You must be an LADWP residential customer with active electric meter service.
  • Application must be completed after both purchasing and installing your high efficiency product. All applications must be postmarked within 12 months of the purchase date.

Please see the list of qualifying items, required documentation, and minimum technical requirements below before you apply. LADWP reserves the right to inspect and verify the installation of your products prior to releasing your rebate check.

How to apply

Customers have two options for submitting their application: online or by mail.

Online

Online Application
  • Log in to your LADWP online account.
  • Upload all supporting documents. Legible electronic copies (PDFs) and photographs (image files) are acceptable.

Mail

Consumer Rebate Program Application pdf

Consumer Rebate Program Application (PDF form to download, print, and mail in)

  • Review and complete Parts A and B, print, and sign the application.

  • Mail the completed rebate application, a copy of your LADWP bill (Page 1), original proof of purchase (with manufacturer, product make and model number), and any additional required documentation to the CRP Processing Center at the address listed on the application.

  • For your records, we advise you to make a copy of all documents (including application) before mailing.

List of Qualifying Items, Required Documentation, And Minimum Technical Requirements

Cool Roof

CRP Rebate $0.20-$0.60 per square foot

Cool Roofs must meet the three-year Solar Reflectance Index (SRI) according to LA Green Building Code. SRI is rated by the Cool Roof Rating Council (CRRC). Rated cool roof products are assigned a CRRC product ID. See www.coolroofs.org.

Additional Documentation

  • PAID Itemized Invoice with CRRC* Product ID, Make/Model, Sq Ft, Slope, Color
  • Final Approved Building and Safety Permit

(Permit not required for liquid applied coating)

Minimum Technical Requirements

  • Low roof slope (≤2:12)
    • 3-Year SRI* ≥ 78 is eligible for a $0.20 per/sq. ft. rebate
    • 3-Year SRI* ≥ 85 is eligible for a $0.60 per/sq. ft. rebate
  • Steep roof slope (>2:12)
    • 3-Year SRI* ≥ 20 is eligible for a $0.20 per/sq. ft. rebate
    • 3-Year SRI* ≥ 20 is eligible for a $0.20 per/sq. ft. rebate 

The rebate is paid per square foot of eligible cool roof product purchased and installed over conditioned space*.

Unconditioned space such as patios, porches, breezeways, carports, or overhangs are not eligible and excluded.

Multi-story buildings - The lower floor(s) under the top story do not add to the total eligible square footage.

*Conditioned space is defined as an enclosed area within a building that is heated or cooled and designed, or modified, to have a complete and effective pressure boundary.

ENERGY STAR® Qualified Residential Window

CRP Rebate $2 per square foot

U-Factor measures how well a window, door, or skylight insulates. Solar heat gain coefficient (SHGC) is the fraction of solar radiation admitted through a window, door, or skylight -- either transmitted directly and/or absorbed, and subsequently released as heat inside a home. The National Fenestration Rating Council (NFRC) label lists the energy performance ratings for energy-efficient windows, doors, and skylights.

Additional Documentation

  • PAID Itemized Invoice with quantity of Windows and Dimensions
  • Manufacturer order confirmation sheet that lists the U-factor*, SHGC*, and dimensions for each window or NFRC* label for each window
  • Final Approved Building and Safety Permit
     

Minimum Technical Requirements

  • Windows/glass doors
    • U-Factor ≤ 0.30
  • SHGC ≤ 0.25
    • Skylights
    • U-Factor ≤ 0.50
    • SHGC ≤ 0.28
  • New construction, additions and Accessory Dwelling Units (ADUs) are not eligible

Heating Ventilation and Air Conditioning (HVAC) System

CRP Rebate $100-$120 per ton

The Air-Conditioning, Heating, and Refrigeration Institute (AHRI) administers a voluntary certification program that provides assurance that HVAC systems perform according to manufacturer’s published claims.

Additional Documentation

  • PAID Itemized Invoice with Make/Model & Matching AHRI* Certificate Reference Number
  • AHRI Certificate of product rating with matching system components
  • Final Approved Building and Safety Permit

Minimum Technical Requirements

  • Central or Split HVAC
    • 15.2 SEER2 rating is eligible for a $100/ton rebate
    • 16.0 SEER2 rating is eligible for a $120/ton rebate
  • Homes or multi-residential dwellings may be eligible for a rebate on more than unit, if applied for at one time.

Related programs

  • Attic Insulation Rebate Program

    The Attic Insulation Rebate Program helps residential customers and property owners improve home comfort and reduces cooling costs by offering rebates for attic insulation. Adding attic insulation is one of the most effective ways to improve your home’s energy efficiency.

    Learn more about Attic Insulation Rebate Program

  • Efficient Product Marketplace (EPM)

    The EPM is a convenient online marketplace that allows you to shop a selection of popular energy-efficient brands available at numerous stores and online retailers with pricing and available rebate information.

    Learn more about Efficient Product Marketplace (EPM)

  • Feed-in Tariff (FiT) Program

    The FiT program allows property owners and developers to sell the output of local eligible renewable energy projects directly to LADWP (as opposed to consuming the energy onsite to satisfy the customer’s load).

    Learn more about Feed-in Tariff (FiT) Program
